# Survey Paper Type

Use this profile for papers whose main contribution is a literature survey, taxonomy, research map,
comparative synthesis, challenge analysis, or future research agenda.

## Use Only As Section Planning Reference

This file is only a section and page-budget reference for building a Paper Framework. It is not a
fixed paper template and does not prescribe writing style, reviewer strategy, claims, experiments,
or citations.

Adapt sections and page budgets to the actual paper, venue, evidence, and user request. Do not copy
this structure mechanically. Merge, rename, split, shrink, or remove sections when the confirmed
venue, contribution, evidence package, or page budget requires it.

## Section And Page-Budget Reference

Survey papers often exceed short conference length. If no venue-specific budget is known, plan a
12-20 page main-text survey by default. Treat 12 pages as a compressed survey/tutorial target and
15-20 pages as the normal starting range for a broad survey. Compress below this range only after
the venue budget is confirmed.

| Order | Candidate section | Typical budget | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Abstract | 0.2-0.3 page | State scope, literature range, taxonomy or organizing lens, key bottlenecks, and future directions. |
| 1 | Introduction | 1.5-2.5 pages | Explain motivation, why another survey is needed, novelty of the organizing perspective, and contributions. |
| 2 | Scope and Methodology | 1.0-2.0 pages | Define search scope, inclusion/exclusion criteria, corpus construction, and taxonomy construction method when available. |
| 3 | Taxonomy / Thematic Sections | 6.0-10.0 pages | Organize literature by taxonomy, technical lineage, architecture family, task setting, application scenario, or evaluation dimension. |
| 4 | Comparative Summary | 1.5-2.5 pages | Use summary tables or structured comparisons to expose strengths, limits, assumptions, and coverage across research lines. |
| 5 | Challenges and Future Directions | 1.5-3.0 pages | Identify unresolved problems, methodological gaps, theory gaps, evaluation needs, and future research agenda. |
| 6 | Conclusion | 0.4-0.7 page | Restate the survey's organizing framework and its practical or research implications. |
| End | Limitations | 0.3-0.6 page | Discuss search coverage, database/language limitations, fast-moving areas, and areas intentionally not covered. |
| Back | Appendix | outside main budget when allowed | Put search strings, PRISMA-style screening details, long paper lists, extra tables, and coding rules here. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- If the venue forces a short survey, combine Scope and Methodology with Introduction and focus the taxonomy on fewer dimensions.
- If the survey is systematic, protect methodology and screening-flow space.
- If the survey is perspective-driven, protect the taxonomy and future-directions sections rather than listing every paper.
