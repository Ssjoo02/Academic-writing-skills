# Position Paper Type

## Story Logic

- Start from a field-level confusion, neglected risk, or misdirected agenda.
- State the thesis, target audience, and decision the paper wants to influence.
- Surface assumptions and define the terms needed for a fair argument.
- Build the case from literature, examples, tradeoffs, and explicit counterarguments.
- End with bounded recommendations and implications, not universal prescriptions.

## Framework Effects

- Abstract: thesis, target debate, evidence base, and practical implication.
- Introduction: define the problem framing and why existing framing is inadequate.
- Argument: make assumptions explicit and separate descriptive claims from normative claims.
- Counterarguments: present strong objections, evidence standards, and where the thesis may fail.
- Implications: translate the argument into research priorities, evaluation standards, or policy choices.
- Related Work: ground the position in adjacent arguments without strawman contrasts.
- Conclusion: state who should act differently and under what conditions.

## Reviewer Risks

- Thesis lacks novelty relative to existing position or survey papers.
- Opposing views are framed as strawmen.
- Prescriptions are unsupported by evidence or argument.
- Important counterarguments are ignored.
- Target audience and intended action are unclear.
