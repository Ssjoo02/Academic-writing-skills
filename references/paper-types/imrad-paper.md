# IMRaD / Empirical Original Research Paper Type

Use this profile for papers whose main contribution is original empirical research organized around
a research question, study design, data collection or experiment, results, and interpretation.
Typical cases include scientific reports, controlled studies, observational studies, user studies,
and systematic empirical analyses where the study and findings are central.

## Use Only As Section Planning Reference

This file is only a section and page-budget reference for building a Paper Framework. It is not a
fixed paper template and does not prescribe writing style, reviewer strategy, claims, experiments,
or citations.

Adapt sections and page budgets to the actual paper, venue, evidence, and user request. Do not copy
this structure mechanically. Merge, rename, split, shrink, or remove sections when the confirmed
venue, contribution, evidence package, or page budget requires it.

## Section And Page-Budget Reference

Assume an 8-10 page main-text research article when no venue-specific budget is known. For journal
submissions, systematic studies, or multi-study empirical papers, plan more space only after the
target venue and evidence package justify it.

| Order | Candidate section | Typical budget | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Abstract | 0.15-0.25 page | State context, research question, study design, main result, and scoped implication. |
| 1 | Introduction | 1.0-1.5 pages | Motivate the problem, summarize the gap, state the research question or hypothesis, and preview the empirical contribution. |
| 2 | Background / Related Work | 0.75-1.25 pages | Provide only the literature and concepts needed to justify the study design and interpret results. |
| 3 | Materials and Methods / Study Design | 1.25-2.0 pages | Define participants, datasets, apparatus, variables, procedures, measures, controls, analysis plan, and reproducibility details. |
| 4 | Results | 1.5-2.5 pages | Present factual findings, statistical or quantitative outcomes, qualitative observations, and figure/table-guided evidence. |
| 5 | Discussion | 1.0-1.75 pages | Interpret findings, connect them to prior work, explain implications, handle alternative explanations, and state limitations. |
| 6 | Conclusion | 0.25-0.5 page | Restate the supported answer to the research question and the practical or scientific takeaway. |
| End | Limitations / Ethics | venue-dependent | State threats to validity, sampling limits, measurement limits, ethical constraints, and generalization boundaries. |
| Back | Appendix | outside main budget when allowed | Put instruments, protocols, extra analyses, screening criteria, extended statistics, and supplementary materials here. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- If Results and Discussion are tightly coupled, combine them as "Results and Discussion" and keep factual reporting distinct from interpretation inside the section.
- If methods are standard and the venue is short, compress Methods but keep enough detail for reproducibility and validity assessment.
- If the paper is a multi-study article, repeat compact Method/Results blocks per study and reserve a final General Discussion.
- If the core contribution is actually a new algorithm, system, benchmark, or survey, use that more specific profile instead.
