# CVPR Venue Profile

## Venue Kind

- conference

## Source Status

- Official sources:
  - CVPR 2026 Author Guidelines: https://cvpr.thecvf.com/Conferences/2026/AuthorGuidelines
  - CVPR 2026 Submission Template: https://github.com/cvpr-org/author-kit/releases
- Access date: 2026-06-08
- Verified for: CVPR 2026 review submission planning.
- Drift risk: high; verify the current author guidelines and template release before submission.

## Scope

- Applies to: CVPR conference papers.
- Submission version: review submission; rebuttal and camera-ready deltas must be rechecked.

## Length And Counting

- Main text limit: 8 pages including figures and tables.
- References count: no, when extra pages contain only cited references.
- Appendix count: no appendix in the main paper is verified; use supplementary material only after checking the current guidelines.
- Checklist count: not verified.
- Ethics/limitations count: not verified as separate page-count exemptions.
- Supplementary material count: optional supplementary material; reviewers are not required to inspect it.

## Post-Main Order

- Required order: main paper, references-only pages.
- Optional sections: supplementary material and one-page rebuttal when the workflow reaches rebuttal.
- Not verified: limitations, ethics, checklist, or appendix placement in the main PDF.

## Drafting Implications

- Use an 8-page Page Budget Summary for all main-text content including figures and tables.
- Put references outside the page budget, but do not move essential evidence into supplementary material.
- Add final checks for anonymous supplementary files, no content-expanding links, no acknowledgments, and no identifying video or repository details.
- Add Open Decisions for compute reporting and ethics discussion when the work uses substantial compute, personal data, or human-subjects data.

## Final Gate

- Before submission-ready: confirm the current official template, 8-page limit, references-only pages, supplementary rules, external-link rules, statement placement, and double-blind requirements.

## Do Not Infer

- Do not infer computer-vision evidence preferences beyond the official submission constraints.
