# Analysis Paper Archetype

## Story Logic

- Start from a phenomenon, failure mode, or empirical pattern that is poorly understood.
- Formulate focused questions and competing explanations before presenting results.
- Design analyses that separate signal from confounds and artifacts.
- Use converging evidence from controls, slices, perturbations, or qualitative inspection.
- Convert findings into bounded explanations and actionable implications.

## Framework Effects

- Abstract: phenomenon, study design, principal findings, limits of interpretation.
- Introduction: explain why the phenomenon matters and what current explanations miss.
- Study Design: state questions, hypotheses, datasets, controls, and threats to validity.
- Analysis: organize around evidence chains, confound checks, robustness tests, and representative cases.
- Discussion: distinguish descriptive findings, causal claims, and practical implications.
- Related Work: connect findings to prior explanations and empirical studies.
- Conclusion: restate the insight and where it may not generalize.

## Reviewer Risks

- Confounds or artifacts explain the observed pattern.
- The paper overclaims causality from observational evidence.
- Examples or slices are cherry-picked.
- Controls are too weak to rule out obvious alternatives.
- Findings have limited generality beyond the studied setting.
