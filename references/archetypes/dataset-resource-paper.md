# Dataset/Resource Paper Archetype

## Story Logic

- Start from a missing resource that blocks measurement, training, or analysis.
- Define the resource's intended users, use cases, and boundaries.
- Explain why existing datasets or resources lack the needed coverage, quality, or access.
- Document how the resource was collected, filtered, annotated, licensed, and maintained.
- Demonstrate utility through representative analyses, baselines, or downstream tasks.

## Framework Effects

- Abstract: resource gap, artifact summary, quality/coverage evidence, intended use.
- Introduction: establish who needs the resource and what prior resources cannot support.
- Resource Construction: detail sourcing, inclusion criteria, annotation, validation, and release format.
- Documentation: provide datasheet-style coverage, limitations, licensing, ethical constraints, and maintenance notes.
- Evaluation: show quality checks, coverage analysis, baseline performance, and downstream utility.
- Related Work: compare resource scope, access conditions, annotation quality, and supported tasks.
- Conclusion: summarize enabled uses and explicit non-uses.

## Reviewer Risks

- Documentation is too thin for reproducible or responsible use.
- Biases are unmeasured or presented as neutral coverage.
- Licensing, consent, privacy, or ethics constraints are unclear.
- Coverage is narrow relative to the claimed use cases.
- Downstream utility is asserted without meaningful demonstrations.
