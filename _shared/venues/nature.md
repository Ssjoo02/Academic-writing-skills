## Venue Kind

- journal

## Source Status

- Official sources: confirm against the current Nature Portfolio author instructions before submission.
- Access date: not verified in this local card.
- Verified for: approximate Nature-family Article planning only.

## Scope

- Applies to: Nature or close Nature-family broad-interest research articles when the user names Nature-family but not Nature Communications.
- Submission version: review submission; final production files may impose additional figure/source-data requirements.

## Length And Counting

- Layout: journal-specific; confirm the active template and whether the submission is Word or LaTeX.
- Main text limit: not verified; use the active author guidelines.
- References count: not verified; Nature-family journals often cap references by article type.
- Abstract / summary paragraph: confirm the active cap and format; draft as a broad-audience, unstructured summary paragraph unless the journal says otherwise.
- Supplementary material count: not verified; distinguish main, Extended Data, and Supplementary Information before drafting.
- Word budget: confirm whether Methods is outside the main text allowance. Nature-style articles commonly place Methods after references, but this must be verified from the active journal instructions. See the `academic-review` skill's `references/checks/journal-submission-elements.md`.

## Blinding

- Confirm current policy for the target Nature-family journal. Do not assume conference-style anonymity.

## Revision Model

- Editorial triage plus peer-review and revision rounds; write for broad scientific importance and technical durability.
- Response-to-reviewers and tracked revision material may be required after review.

## Supplementary And Extension

- Plan main display items, Extended Data, and Supplementary Information as separate evidence tiers.
- Extended Data should support the main story without becoming the only place where central evidence appears.
- For conference-to-journal extension, state what is newly added and why the article now supports a journal-scale claim.
- See `journal-vs-conference.md` for the journal drafting posture.

## Post-Main Order

- Required order: not verified; Nature-style articles may place Methods after References.
- Optional sections: Data Availability, Code Availability, Author Contributions, Competing Interests, Reporting Summary, Extended Data, Supplementary Information, and cover letter or editorial summary material depending on the journal.
- Not verified: exact order, caps, file formats, and whether a separate reporting checklist applies.

## Drafting Implications

- Open with a summary paragraph that starts broad enough for a multidisciplinary audience, narrows to the exact problem, states the finding, and widens back to significance.
- Treat broad multidisciplinary audience as a writing constraint: define subfield terms early and avoid assuming specialist motivation.
- Build the Results narrative around findings and interpretation, not a conference-style experiment inventory.
- Plan Data/Code Availability and source-data obligations at Paper Framework time; unresolved repository identifiers stay blockers or Open Decisions.
- Plan figure tiers before drafting: main figures carry the article argument; Extended Data and Supplementary Information carry support, controls, and completeness.
- Use the `academic-review` skill's `references/checks/high-impact-journal-review.md` before calling the manuscript strong for a Nature-family target.

## Final Gate

- Before submission-ready: verify current Nature instructions for article type, summary paragraph, Methods placement, display-item caps, Extended Data / Supplementary Information, data/code availability, reporting summary, cover letter, and source-data files.

## Do Not Infer

- Do not infer exact word caps, reference caps, figure caps, Methods placement, or reporting forms from memory.
- Do not claim Nature fit as settled; at most report whether the draft supports a broad-interest case under the checked criteria.
