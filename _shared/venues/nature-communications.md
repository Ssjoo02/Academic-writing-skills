# Nature Communications Venue Profile

## Venue Kind

- journal

## Source Status

- Official sources: confirm against the current Nature Communications author instructions before submission.
- Access date: not verified in this local card.
- Verified for: approximate Article / Brief Communication planning based on modeled Nature Communications constraints.
- Drift risk: high; word limits, display-item caps, reporting requirements, and templates can change.

## Scope

- Applies to: Nature Communications Articles and Brief Communications when the user names Nature Communications or Nat Commun.
- Submission version: review submission; production and revision files may add checks.

## Length And Counting

- Layout: journal-specific; confirm current template route.
- Main text limit: approximate planning value for Article is around 5,000 words including Methods; confirm current author instructions.
- References count: confirm current cap and style.
- Abstract limit: approximate planning value for Article is around 150 words, single paragraph, no citations; confirm current instructions.
- Supplementary material count: separate Supplementary Information; Nature Communications does not use a Nature-style Extended Data tier by default.
- Word budget: Methods-in-cap trap. If the active cap includes Methods, budget Methods inside the cap before drafting; do not draft a full body and then append Methods on top. See the `academic-review` skill's `references/checks/journal-submission-elements.md`.

## Blinding

- Confirm current review policy. Do not assume conference-style anonymity.

## Revision Model

- Editorial triage plus peer review and revision rounds; reviewers may see reporting and availability materials.
- Transfers from other Nature-family journals require rechecking format differences, especially Methods placement and display tiers.

## Supplementary And Extension

- Plan up to 10 display items in the main article as an approximate Article cap until verified; figures and tables are counted together.
- Overflow belongs in Supplementary Information, not an Extended Data tier.
- For a prior conference version, state the extension and the new evidence package explicitly.
- See `journal-vs-conference.md` for the journal drafting posture.

## Post-Main Order

- Required order: not verified; confirm the current manuscript template.
- Optional sections: Data Availability, Code Availability, Author Contributions, Competing Interests, Reporting Summary, Supplementary Information, and Cover letter.
- Not verified: exact section order, figure-source-data requirements, and reporting form variants.

## Drafting Implications

- Budget the article before drafting: Introduction, Results, Discussion, and Methods must fit the active word cap when Methods are included.
- Keep the abstract short, finding-led, quantitative where evidence allows, and citation-free.
- Decide main vs Supplementary display items at Paper Framework time; 10 display items is the planning ceiling until the current cap is confirmed.
- Treat Data Availability, Code Availability, Reporting Summary, and cover letter as companion artifacts, not afterthoughts.
- Use the `academic-review` skill's `references/checks/data-code-availability.md` to inventory repositories and identifiers before drafting availability statements.
- Use the `academic-review` skill's `references/checks/high-impact-journal-review.md` for broad-interest and nonspecialist-readability risk.

## Final Gate

- Before submission-ready: verify current Nature Communications instructions for word count, Methods inclusion, abstract cap, reference cap, display-item cap, Reporting Summary, Data/Code Availability, cover letter, template route, and Supplementary Information.

## Do Not Infer

- Do not invent exact current caps or reporting forms from memory.
- Do not treat Nature Communications as interchangeable with Nature; Methods counting, display tiers, and transfer handling may differ.
