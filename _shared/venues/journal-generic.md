# Generic Journal Venue Profile

## Venue Kind

- journal

## Source Status

- Official sources: none specific. This is a fallback card for journals not individually modeled.
- Access date: not applicable.
- Verified for: approximate planning only.
- Drift risk: not applicable; every field below must be confirmed against the target journal's current author guidelines.

## Scope

- Applies to: journal submissions not covered by a specific venue card (for example TMLR, IEEE TNNLS / TIP / TMM, ACM TODS / TOIS, Elsevier, Springer, Wiley titles).
- Submission version: review submission; recheck at revision.

## Length And Counting

- Layout: `not verified` — confirm single vs double column from the target journal template.
- Main text limit: `not verified` — journals vary from no hard limit to strict word/page caps. Ask the user or check the author guidelines.
- References count: `not verified` — confirm whether references count toward any limit.
- Abstract limit: `not verified` — confirm word/character cap and whether it is structured or unstructured.
- Supplementary material count: `not verified` — confirm whether supplementary material is separate and uncapped.
- Word budget: `not verified` — confirm whether the cap is by words or pages and, critically, **whether the word cap includes Methods** (the Methods-in-cap trap). If word-capped, propose a per-section budget before drafting. See the `academic-review` skill's `references/checks/journal-submission-elements.md` (Length And Word Budget).

## Blinding

- `not verified` — confirm single-blind, double-blind, or open review before deciding anonymity and self-citation handling.

## Revision Model

- Assume a major/minor revision cycle with a response-to-reviewers letter unless the journal states otherwise.
- No conference-style camera-ready crunch; write for multi-round review.

## Supplementary And Extension

- Assume supplementary material is allowed as separate files; confirm specifics.
- For a paper extended from a conference version, state the new contribution explicitly and confirm the journal's extension policy (many require a substantial, often ~30%+, new contribution).
- See `journal-vs-conference.md` for the journal drafting posture.

## Post-Main Order

- `not verified` — confirm required section order, required statements (data/code availability, ethics, author contributions, conflicts), and graphical-abstract / highlights requirements from the target journal.

## Drafting Implications

- Do not assume a conference page budget. Until the journal is confirmed, plan a complete, self-contained article and record the length/format constraints as Open Decisions.
- Ask the user once for: target journal, single vs double column, word/page limit, abstract format and cap, reference style, blinding, and required submission elements (graphical abstract, highlights, key points, data/code availability).
- If the user later names a specifically modeled journal (JMLR, TPAMI), switch to that card rather than guessing.

## Final Gate

- Before submission-ready: confirm the target journal's current author guidelines for length, abstract, references, blinding, required statements, supplementary handling, and template, and resolve every `not verified` field above.

## Do Not Infer

- Do not invent length limits, abstract caps, reference style, blinding, or required statements from memory.
- Do not infer acceptance bar, reviewer tastes, or scope.
