# Journal Overlay: Introduction

Apply on top of `references/sections/introduction.md`. Keep the base logic map,
the base Section Skeleton, the Task/Challenge/Pipeline template versions, the compact
contribution-bullet rule, and the quality checklist. Adjust for journals as follows.

## Non-Anonymous: Build On Your Own Work Directly

Journal review is single-blind / non-anonymous for the standard tracks (confirm
from the venue card's `Blinding`). The Introduction may cite and build on the
authors' own prior work by name, without the anonymized phrasing a conference
Introduction needs.

For a paper extended from a conference version, state the new contribution over
the prior version **explicitly in the Introduction** (what is added, why it is
substantial). JMLR and TPAMI expect a clear, non-trivial extension; do not bury
this in a footnote.

## Fuller, But Still Funneled, Motivation

Journals carry less page pressure than conferences, so a broader motivation and a
more thorough prior-work treatment are expected. This is permission for
completeness, not for a literature dump: keep the controlled funnel from the base
guide (field stake → bottleneck → fair prior work → remaining gap → this study as
the direct response). Do not trade the narrowing logic for breadth.

## Frame A Complete Study, Not A Single Trick

Conference intros often sell one novel contribution. A journal Introduction should
set up a **complete, self-contained study**: the full problem, the scope of what
this paper resolves, and the boundary. Contribution bullets should describe a
coherent body of work, each bullet still one compact claim sentence (base rule),
not a four-part mini-paragraph or a component inventory. State the study-level scope
and boundary in the prose blocks, not by inflating every bullet.

## Audience Width By Venue

- Field journals (JMLR, TPAMI): keep the base technical-challenge funnel; readers
  are specialists.
- Broad-audience / high-impact venues: widen the opening so a cross-disciplinary
  reader can enter, then narrow to the exact gap (`broad field → sharper
  background → exact gap → this study`). The contribution still arrives as a clear
  hinge, just with more accessible framing before it.

## Roadmap Paragraph (Journal Add-On)

The base (conference) Introduction has no roadmap paragraph. Journal articles are
longer, so a short roadmap is a journal-specific addition that is often warranted
here. When used, append it after the contributions and any evidence preview, keep it
to 3-5 sentences, and say what each section contributes to the argument; do not just
list section numbers.

Sentence skeleton:

1. `The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section 2 reviews ... Section 3
   describes ... Section 4 presents ... Section 5 concludes with limitations and
   future directions.`

## Quality Add-Ons (Journal)

In addition to the base checklist:

1. If extended from a conference paper, is the new-contribution statement explicit
   and substantial?
2. Does the Introduction frame a complete study with a stated boundary, not a
   single isolated trick?
3. Is prior-work coverage thorough yet still funneled toward the exact gap?
