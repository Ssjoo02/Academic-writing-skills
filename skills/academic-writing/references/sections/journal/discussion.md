# Journal Overlay: Discussion and Conclusion

Apply on top of `references/sections/conclusion.md`. The base guide treats the
closing as a compact Conclusion/Limitations block, which fits conferences that
fold interpretation into the Conclusion. Journals usually split these. Adjust as
follows.

## Journals Often Want A Standalone Discussion

Most journal articles carry a dedicated **Discussion** section before a short
Conclusion, where conferences often merge the two. Add a Discussion when the
venue and paper type expect one (survey and position papers may instead use a
Trends/Open-Questions section; theory papers may use a shorter discussion). Use
this movement:

1. Restate the central advance.
2. Explain why the evidence supports it.
3. Show how it changes a workflow, design rule, or conceptual boundary.
4. Relate it to previous studies fairly and specifically.
5. State the limits and dependencies that remain.
6. Name the future work that is now plausible.

Do not restate every figure. Select the evidence that changes interpretation.

## Limitations Are Substantive, Not A Formality

Journal review is multi-round and thorough. Treat Limitations as a real
subsection: name the boundary conditions, failure modes, and dependencies
honestly. A thin limitations paragraph invites reviewer pushback across rounds.

## Keep The Conclusion Compact

After the Discussion, keep the Conclusion to the base four-part close (main
contribution → decisive evidence → broader implication → boundary). The
Conclusion introduces no new data, citations, or mechanisms — anything new belongs
in Results or Discussion.

## Placement Per Venue

Follow the active venue card's `Post-Main Order` (for example TPAMI: Body →
Conclusion → Appendix → Acknowledgment → References). Do not hard-code a single
order.

## Quality Add-Ons (Journal)

In addition to the base checklist:

1. Is interpretation in a Discussion that widens from finding to meaning, rather
   than crammed into the Conclusion?
2. Are limitations explicit, specific, and proportionate to the claims?
3. Does the Conclusion stay compact and introduce nothing new?
